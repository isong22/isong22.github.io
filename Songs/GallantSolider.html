The Gallant Soldier - A


Come all you lads of high renown,

That will hear of a fair young maiden

And she roved out of a summer’s day

For to view the soldiers parading.

They marched so bold, they looked so gay

The colors fine and the bands did play

And it caused young Mary for to say,

“I’ll wed me a Gallant Soldier.”


She viewed the soldiers on parade

And as they stood at their leisure,

And Mary to her self did say,

“At last I find my treasure.

But oh how cruel me parents must be

To banish my true love away from me,

We’ll leave them all, and I’ll go with thee

Me bold and un-daunted soldier.”


Oh Mary dear, your parent’s love

I pray don’t be unruly,

For when you’re in a foreign land,

Believe you’ll rue it surely.

Perhaps in battle I might fa-all

From a shot, from angry cannon ball

And you’re, so far from your daddy’s hall,

Be advised by a gallant soldier.
